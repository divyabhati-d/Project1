1. Fender could not play the guitar:

When you hear the name, Fender, you immediately recognize him as the founder of the guitar. But, what if I say that the founder of the instrument, Leo Fender, actually never played the instrument? He did not even learn to play the guitar. Fender started his career by repairing phonographs, radios, and other electronics. In the 1940s, Fender started manufacturing guitars as a different type of instruments. Later in the 1950s, they launched the first mass-produced electric guitar.

2. Music synchs with your heartbeats:

Experts say that music is directly linked to the activities of the different parts of our body, especially the cardiovascular system. In research, it was found earlier that hearts respond differently to different beats and tempos of music. Moreover, the brain cells also respond to music quickly. It also shows how a distorted piece of music can increase heartbeats and increase blood pressure.
